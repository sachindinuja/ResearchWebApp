Literature Survey

Recent studies have highlighted the growing influence of digital platforms in shaping global tea consumption trends. Tools like Google Trends provide insights into seasonal and regional variations in consumer interest, while social media platforms such as Instagram, TikTok, and Twitter have become key channels for promoting tea culture, especially among younger demographics. However, existing research often examines these platforms in isolation, lacking a holistic approach that integrates search engine data with social media discourse for deeper market insights. Seasonal demand for tea in Sri Lanka is further influenced by climate variability, auction trends, and global economic factors. Although prior studies emphasize the importance of environmental and economic conditions, they rarely incorporate comprehensive models that unify sales data, elevation-specific segmentation, and predictive analytics. On the export front, macroeconomic indicators‚Äîparticularly the Consumer Price Index (CPI)‚Äîare recognized predictors of international demand. Advanced forecasting models like Random Forest and LSTM have shown strong performance in capturing non-linear economic patterns, but their application in Sri Lankan tea export forecasting remains limited. Furthermore, while traditional models such as ARIMA have been used in production forecasting, newer machine learning techniques like XGBoost and Support Vector Machines offer greater accuracy, especially in handling regional variations in yield due to climate differences. This study addresses these research gaps by integrating digital trends, economic indicators, and machine learning-based predictive methods for a comprehensive analysis of Sri Lanka‚Äôs tea industry.


Research Gap

Despite advancements in predictive modeling within the tea industry, several critical research gaps remain unaddressed: Lack of Integrated Digital Trend Analysis: Most existing studies examine search trends and social media discussions separately. There is a noticeable absence of a unified framework that combines these digital sources to offer a more comprehensive view of consumer behavior. Limited Segmentation in Seasonal Demand Forecasting: While previous research acknowledges the impact of climate and pricing on tea demand, it often overlooks elevation-based segmentation. This restricts the accuracy of forecasting models, particularly in a geographically diverse region like Sri Lanka. Underutilization of CPI in Export Forecasting: Although the Consumer Price Index (CPI) is a well-known indicator of international trade performance, it has rarely been applied in conjunction with machine learning models like Random Forest to forecast Sri Lankan tea exports. This study addresses that by introducing a CPI-driven, tea-type-specific forecasting approach. Need for Enhanced Tea Production Forecasting by Category: Current studies have explored machine learning in climate-based yield predictions but have not fully leveraged ensemble learning techniques for optimized results across different tea-growing zones. This research contributes by consolidating insights across digital trend analysis, economic indicators, seasonal forecasting, and production modeling. It introduces a data-driven methodology that utilizes machine learning and cross-disciplinary data integration to improve forecasting accuracy in the Sri Lankan tea sector. Future work should explore holistic models that merge search trends, social media engagement, macroeconomic factors, and environmental data for more precise and actionable predictions.
Incorporating real-time feedback mechanisms for continual refinement is also a crucial yet underexplored aspect. Moreover, limited studies have delved into the development of dynamic algorithms that can adaptively recommend the most suitable assessment approaches based on contextual factors such as subject matter, student performance, and learning objectives. There is also a dearth of research on seamlessly incorporating technology-enhanced assessments into the recommendation process. Furthermore, the existing research overlooks the need for comprehensive student profiles that encompass academic performance, interests, extracurricular activities, and career aspirations. Aligning subject stream recommendations with a student's envisioned long-term career trajectory is an unexplored territory that holds potential for significant impact.

Research Methodology

The methodology encompasses a multi-faceted approach to address the challenges in enhancing English vocabulary skills among Grade 6 students through innovative technologies and educational strategies.

For evaluating vocabulary proficiency and crafting tailored learning paths, machine learning, natural language processing, and educational technology are integrated to categorize students, personalize learning paths, and dynamically adjust exercises based on proficiency levels.

Leveraging handwriting recognition involves dataset preparation, model training using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), Flask-based API development, and React-based frontend interface design for a mobile application, enhancing vocabulary writing skills through real-time feedback.

Facial expression detection for engagement employs datasets for emotion detection, Deep Face library for facial attribute analysis, and mathematical algorithms for attention monitoring. Real-time monitoring captures data from device cameras to categorize emotions and monitor attention fluctuations.

Advancing English vocabulary learning through voice recognition technology involves selecting a vocabulary set, collecting voice data, training a voice recognition model using CNN algorithms, and deploying the model within a mobile application's infrastructure. Pronunciation practice activities, and quizzes with real-time feedback engage Grade 6 students in immersive learning experiences.
