Literature Survey

Recent studies have highlighted the growing influence of digital platforms in shaping global tea consumption trends. Tools like Google Trends provide insights into seasonal and regional variations in consumer interest, while social media platforms such as Instagram, TikTok, and Twitter have become key channels for promoting tea culture, especially among younger demographics. However, existing research often examines these platforms in isolation, lacking a holistic approach that integrates search engine data with social media discourse for deeper market insights. Seasonal demand for tea in Sri Lanka is further influenced by climate variability, auction trends, and global economic factors. Although prior studies emphasize the importance of environmental and economic conditions, they rarely incorporate comprehensive models that unify sales data, elevation-specific segmentation, and predictive analytics. On the export front, macroeconomic indicators—particularly the Consumer Price Index (CPI)—are recognized predictors of international demand. Advanced forecasting models like Random Forest and LSTM have shown strong performance in capturing non-linear economic patterns, but their application in Sri Lankan tea export forecasting remains limited. Furthermore, while traditional models such as ARIMA have been used in production forecasting, newer machine learning techniques like XGBoost and Support Vector Machines offer greater accuracy, especially in handling regional variations in yield due to climate differences. This study addresses these research gaps by integrating digital trends, economic indicators, and machine learning-based predictive methods for a comprehensive analysis of Sri Lanka’s tea industry.


Research Gap

Despite advancements in predictive modeling within the tea industry, several critical research gaps remain unaddressed: Lack of Integrated Digital Trend Analysis: Most existing studies examine search trends and social media discussions separately. There is a noticeable absence of a unified framework that combines these digital sources to offer a more comprehensive view of consumer behavior. Limited Segmentation in Seasonal Demand Forecasting: While previous research acknowledges the impact of climate and pricing on tea demand, it often overlooks elevation-based segmentation. This restricts the accuracy of forecasting models, particularly in a geographically diverse region like Sri Lanka. Underutilization of CPI in Export Forecasting: Although the Consumer Price Index (CPI) is a well-known indicator of international trade performance, it has rarely been applied in conjunction with machine learning models like Random Forest to forecast Sri Lankan tea exports. This study addresses that by introducing a CPI-driven, tea-type-specific forecasting approach. Need for Enhanced Tea Production Forecasting by Category: Current studies have explored machine learning in climate-based yield predictions but have not fully leveraged ensemble learning techniques for optimized results across different tea-growing zones. This research contributes by consolidating insights across digital trend analysis, economic indicators, seasonal forecasting, and production modeling. It introduces a data-driven methodology that utilizes machine learning and cross-disciplinary data integration to improve forecasting accuracy in the Sri Lankan tea sector. Future work should explore holistic models that merge search trends, social media engagement, macroeconomic factors, and environmental data for more precise and actionable predictions.
Incorporating real-time feedback mechanisms for continual refinement is also a crucial yet underexplored aspect. Moreover, limited studies have delved into the development of dynamic algorithms that can adaptively recommend the most suitable assessment approaches based on contextual factors such as subject matter, student performance, and learning objectives. There is also a dearth of research on seamlessly incorporating technology-enhanced assessments into the recommendation process. Furthermore, the existing research overlooks the need for comprehensive student profiles that encompass academic performance, interests, extracurricular activities, and career aspirations. Aligning subject stream recommendations with a student's envisioned long-term career trajectory is an unexplored territory that holds potential for significant impact.

Research Problem & Solution

The research addresses several key challenges in the Sri Lankan tea industry:

❓ Lack of Digital Consumer Insights: Traditional market research fails to capture rapidly evolving consumer preferences and trends in tea consumption.

❓ Seasonal Demand Variability: Tea demand fluctuates significantly throughout the year, affected by climate, elevation, and market factors, making accurate forecasting difficult.

❓ Export Market Uncertainty: Tea exporters struggle to predict international demand due to complex economic factors and changing consumer preferences in different countries.

❓ Production Planning Challenges: Tea producers need better forecasting tools to optimize production across different tea categories (Orthodox, CTC, and Green tea).

Our solution implements advanced machine learning models that integrate multiple data sources (social media trends, historical sales, economic indicators, and climate data) to create comprehensive forecasting systems for each aspect of the tea industry supply chain, enabling more informed decision-making for stakeholders.

Research Objectives

Analyze tea demand in Sri Lanka using data-driven insights from social media and google trends

Analyze how the demand for tea fluctuates throughout the year due to sales factors

Develop a data-driven model using economic indicators and export history to accurately forecast tea export demand across countries, aiding strategic decisions for exporters

Develop a predictive model that accurately forecasts local market release tea production in Sri Lanka, distinguishing between Orthodox tea, CTC tea and green tea categories

Research Methodology

A. Analysis of Tea Trends via Google Trends and Social Media
This study uses a data-driven approach to analyze tea trends across platforms like Google Trends, Instagram, and Facebook. By extracting data through APIs, the project identifies popular tea types, seasonal patterns, and engagement behavior. Preprocessing steps included data cleaning, timestamp standardization, and hashtag categorization. Time-series analysis methods were applied to uncover trend shifts and consumer interest over time.

B. Tea Demand Forecasting Based on Sales and Elevation
Machine learning models were applied to analyze seasonal demand fluctuations using historical sales data from the Sri Lanka Tea Board. The data was segmented by elevation (high-, mid-, and low-grown tea) and processed with techniques like PCA and mutual information for feature selection. Among several models tested, Random Forest achieved the highest accuracy (R² = 0.907). Bias mitigation and seasonal adjustments ensured balanced, reliable predictions.

C. Predicting Tea Export Demand Using Economic Indicators
Using macroeconomic data such as CPI from key importing countries, this study employed supervised learning models to forecast Sri Lanka's tea exports. Historical export data and economic indicators were cleaned, normalized, and engineered into lag features. After testing models like Linear Regression and K-Nearest Neighbors, Random Forest again performed best (R² = 0.910), capturing complex relationships between economic conditions and export volumes.

D. Category-Wise Tea Production Forecasting (Orthodox, CTC, Green)
To model tea production trends, data from 2011–2022 was collected by tea type and elevation. Environmental factors like temperature, rainfall, and humidity were included. After preprocessing and scaling, eight machine learning models were tested. A custom ensemble model combining Random Forest, Extra Trees, and LightGBM delivered the most accurate predictions (R² > 0.91). The model's performance was validated using MAE, RMSE, and R² metrics.
